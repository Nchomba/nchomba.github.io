Project · Healthcare Analytics & Revenue Cycle Management

Hospital Transaction Dashboard

Built an end-to-end Power BI dashboard analysing hospital billing transactions, patient payment behaviour, and revenue cycle performance. The dashboard gave finance and operations teams real-time visibility into cash flow, claim denials, and outstanding balances — enabling faster collections and improved financial planning.

Business Problem

The hospital finance team had no centralised view of billing performance. Claim denial rates were rising, outstanding AR was growing, and department-level revenue variances were unknown — all managed through disconnected Excel sheets.

Key Findings
  • 27% of insurance claims were denied on first submission — top reason: missing patient authorisation codes
  • Outpatient billing accounted for 62% of total revenue but had the highest outstanding AR (45-day avg collection period)
  • 3 departments (Radiology, Pathology, Emergency) drove 71% of uncollected balances
  • Cash pay patients had a 38% higher on-time payment rate than insured patients
  • Revenue peaked in Q1 and Q3 — correlated with elective procedure scheduling cycles
  • SSIS ETL reduced manual data entry by ~14 hours/week by automating feeds from 4 source systems
Recommendations Delivered
  • Implement pre-authorisation checklist to reduce claim denials by est. 40%
  • Set up automated payment reminders at 14, 30, 45-day intervals for outstanding AR
  • Create department-level collection KPI targets tracked in monthly SSRS report
  • Introduce cash-pay discount programme to incentivise upfront payments
  • Dashboard alert when denial rate exceeds 15% threshold in any department
